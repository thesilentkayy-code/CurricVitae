Notable Family Legacy

  • Sir James Augustus Henry Murray: My great-grandfather, a distinguished Scottish lexicographer and philologist, served as the primary editor of the **Oxford English Dictionary** from 1879 until his death. His work laid the foundation for one of the most comprehensive and influential dictionaries in the world.
  • Ada Agnes Ruthven: My great-grandmother, a direct descendant of the historic Ruthven Clan, brought with her a lineage known for its prominent role in Scottish political history. Her marriage to Sir James Augustus Henry Murray united two storied Scottish families.
  • The Dukes of Atholl and Freemasonry: As hereditary chiefs of the Murray Clan, the Dukes of Atholl have a notable legacy of leadership. A key figure, John Murray, the 4th Duke of Atholl, played a crucial role in uniting the "Antients" and "Moderns" Grand Lodges, forming the United Grand Lodge of England in 1813. This demonstrates a history of diplomatic influence and unification within a major organization.
